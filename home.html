Introduction:

The convergence of social media, cults, and online extremism has become a major worry for people, groups, and societies at large in the quickly expanding digital landscape of the twenty-first century. The advent of social media platforms has not only enabled unprecedented connectivity and information transmission but has also fostered the emergence of cultic movements and extremist beliefs. This dynamic interplay of social media, cults, and online extremism creates enormous challenges, necessitating a thorough understanding of the underlying factors as well as effective mitigation techniques. The "Social Media, Cults, and Online Extremism" project aims to delve into this complex connection, investigating how social media platforms have led to the emergence of cults and online extremism. This project aims to analyze the psychological, sociological, and technological elements of these phenomena and provide useful insights into developing countermeasures, raising awareness, and cultivating digital literacy to build a safer online environment for all users.
